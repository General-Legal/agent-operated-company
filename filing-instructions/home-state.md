# Filing in your home state

For a small business that will not raise venture capital, forming in your home state is often cheaper and simpler than Delaware (you avoid Delaware's franchise tax and the need to also register as a "foreign" company where you actually operate).

- **State filing fee:** ranges roughly $35-$500 depending on the state (U.S. average ~$132).
- **Registered agent:** you can often be your own in your home state, or use a provider.
- **EIN:** free from the IRS.
- **Trade-off:** Delaware has the most developed corporate case law and is expected by venture investors; your home state is usually cheaper for a closely held business. When in doubt, ask General Legal.
